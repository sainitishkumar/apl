There are  houses located in a straight line. The first house is numbered  and the last one is numbered . Every house has a small front garden. Daily water suppliment is needed in order to maintain these gardens. But, problem is only  houses out of these  have the water sprinklers.

Each sprinkler has an equal range. Sprinkled water can reach upto  gardens in both directions including garden where it's present. So, the total range of single water sprinkler is + gardens.

Assume that we are given the  locations (out of ) in which the sprinklers are present.

Now, we want to find out total number of front house gardens which are out of the reach of water.

Note - There exists a trivial solution for above problem, but it is not enough to pass all test cases. So, the goal is to improve the trivial solution to a linear time solution.

In second case, sprinkler at house number  will cover the gardens from house number  to . Similarly, sprinkler at house number  will cover gardens from house number  to . So, total  gardens from house number  to  will not get the water.
